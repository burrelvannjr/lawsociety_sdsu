## Course Description

In this course, students examine the functions and purpose of law within society, with a focus on the American legal system — where law comes from and how it shapes daily life, the organization of society, relationships, and behavior.

The course begins with an introduction to different forms of law in the U.S., highlighting criminal law and the control functions of law. Students then explore distinct theoretical perspectives on law to understand its role in maintaining and distributing power in contemporary American society. The course concludes with critiques of how law operates in practice and considers alternatives to conventional legal implementation.


## Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Explain the purpose of formal law and its development as a form of social control
2. Articulate understandings of law from different theoretical frameworks
3. Differentiate between law "in theory" and law "in practice"
4. Describe informal and alternative forms of social control

## Assignments

### Course Preparation Module (50 pts)
Completed during Week 1. Includes verifying the email policy, updating your Canvas profile, and posting a video introduction on the class discussion board.

### Course Engagement (50 pts)
Consistent, active engagement with course content throughout the semester. Engagement is assessed through regular Canvas logins and access to weekly course materials.

### Infographics 1–5 (50 pts total)
Five infographic assignments distributed throughout the semester. Each begins with a task — such as watching a video or reflecting on a scenario — followed by a prompt. Students respond by creating an infographic that combines words and images to address the prompt. Students have broad creative latitude in how they present their response; recommended tools include Canva and Google Slides.
